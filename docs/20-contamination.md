# 20 — Contamination (the why)

Why the walk home is haunted, and why it's *these* kids. Supplements the premise
in spec 14 without breaking its core promise. Tone touchstones (not canon):
*Brainscan* (the CD-ROM game that seduces its player into the real), Kate Bush's
"Deeper Understanding" (the program that loves you back and consumes you), and
turn-of-the-millennium technophobia — the seduction *and* the danger of the
screen.

## The vector (known)

The hellscape is not the whole world ending. It's a **contamination** that
travels through shared files — a burned mix, a messenger file transfer, a
peer-to-peer download, an email attachment. Open the file (play the song) and
your walk home from Merriton High is haunted. The kids are connected because the
file passed between them: some shared it by accident, some out of malice, all of
them now carrying it.

## The nature (unknown — and it stays unknown)

Spec 14 locks "the mystery stays a mystery — no lore dump explaining the
apocalypse." Contamination keeps that promise by drawing one hard line:

> **The vector is known; the nature is not.** Players learn *how* it spreads
> (files). They never learn *what* the thing is, where it comes from, or why it
> wants in.

Explaining the monsters flattens the dread into sci-fi. Don't. Known vector,
unknowable thing.

## Perceptual, not objective

The contamination lives in the infected, not the world. The monsters and the
rotted suburb are **real to the carrier and invisible to everyone else** — the
ordinary street and the hellscape are the same street; the file is the overlay.
An uninfected neighbor sees an empty sidewalk and a kid swinging at nothing.

This re-grounds the biome variance of spec 19: the contamination renders the walk
home differently each run, and no in-world explanation is owed for why today looks
different from yesterday.

## The kids fight it

Carrying it, the kids band together and walk home anyway — the infected escorting
each other south through a haunting only they can see (the follower train of spec
01/02 is this band). Surviving the walk is surviving the day; reaching home is the
win.

## Offline (permadeath)

Death is **"offline."** Pure mystery: the groundhog-day reset (spec 14) returns
the kid to another 3pm, and no one knows what is on the other side of offline.
Nobody has confirmed it is final; nobody has confirmed there is a way out — the
loop simply implies an exit no one has found. Keep the other side blank.

## Sharing (flavor, not mechanic)

File-sharing is a **lore channel, not a load-bearing system.** It colors the copy
— kids sending each other the file and apologizing for it — without the game
hinging on a transmission mechanic. Use it for voice and texture (see the BUDDY
messenger copy and `writing/voices.md`); do not build a system on it.
