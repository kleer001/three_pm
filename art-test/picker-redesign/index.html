3PM — Hero Picker · four in-idiom directions

Same real roster & data, four visions of “2000s electronic-music raver, but clean.” Each embraces the era's neon/gradient/chrome as a deliberate signature (not AI-slop), held together by a strict grid, clear hierarchy, and negative space.

01 · CLINICAL

Techno Readout

The Designers Republic × Pentagram. The Warp Records / WipEout idiom: near-black engineering grid, monospace data, big unit numerals, technical glyphs, one acid accent. Built for dense info.
02 · GLOSSY

Y2K Aqua

Frutiger Aero × Mac OS X Aqua. Early-2000s optimism: glassy translucent panels, candy gradients, gel buttons, glossy orbs, soft bloom. The one light-mode take — bright and shiny.
03 · FLUORO

UV Rave

Acid-house flyer, disciplined. Pure black, fluoro UV palette, heavy condensed type, strobe-disc + halftone. All the flyer energy, locked to a strict grid so it reads clean, not cluttered.
04 · PLAYER-SKIN

Winamp / Spectrum

MP3-era player skin. Beveled chrome, LCD readouts, a graphic-EQ for stats, a hero “playlist,” spectrum analyzer. The most literally on-theme — the menu IS a music player.
